## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

17. **Ignoring the Landscape**
    Mistake: Not considering slopes, soil types, or drainage issues can lead to unstable fences. | Tip: Plan your fence design to suit the land; adjust heights or use stepped panels if needed.

18. **Shallow Post Holes**
    Mistake: Fence posts that aren't set deep enough can lean or fall over, especially in high winds. | Tip: Dig post holes at least one-third of the post's length deep—usually around 600mm to 900mm.

19. **Incorrect Post Spacing**
    Mistake: Spacing posts too far apart can cause panels to sag or buckle. | Tip: Keep posts evenly spaced, generally 2.4m apart, depending on the fence style.

20. **Ignoring Wind Load**
    Mistake: Solid fences without gaps can act like sails, catching strong winds and tipping over. | Tip: Use designs like slatted fences to allow airflow while maintaining privacy!

21. **Not Planning for Maintenance**
    Mistake: Assuming a fence is 'set and forget' can lead to early deterioration. | Tip: Schedule regular checks for loose posts, rust, or rot, and carry out minor repairs early.

## ⚖️ Pros and Cons

60. **Colorbond**
    Pros: Durable, Low-maintenance, Rust-resistant, Stylish, Secure
    Cons: Expensive, Rigid, Noisy, Heat-absorbing, Denting

61. **Timber Fencing**
    Pros: Natural look, Affordable, Customisable with paint or stain, Blends well with gardens, Readily available
    Cons: Requires maintenance, Prone to termites, Shorter lifespan, Can warp or rot, Needs sealing or painting

62. **Chain Wire Fencing**
    Pros: Affordable, Allows airflow, Easy to install, Good for security, Can be reinforced with barbed wire
    Cons: No privacy, Can rust over time, Not very durable, Can sag if not tensioned, Industrial look

63. **PVC Fencing**
    Pros: No painting required, Weather-resistant, Easy to clean, Won't rot or rust, Long lifespan
    Cons: Can fade in extreme sun, More expensive than timber, Less impact-resistant, Can crack in cold, Limited colour choices

64. **Fencing for Security**
    Pros: Intruder deterrent, Safe for pets, Added property value, Lockable gates, Customisable
    Cons: Unwelcoming look, Creates blind spots, High cost, Neighbour disputes, Ongoing maintenance
