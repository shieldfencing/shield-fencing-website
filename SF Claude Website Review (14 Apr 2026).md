* FAQ  
  * Do I need council approval for a retaining wall in Brisbane?  
    * Retaining walls under 1m in height typically do not require approval.  
    * Requirements can vary depending on overlays such as flood, landslide, or heritage. We can advise based on your property.  
  * What is the 1m height limit about?  
    * We specialise in retaining walls up to 1m in height (approximately 5 sleepers).  
    * Walls above this height require engineering design and are not within our scope.  
  * How much does a retaining wall cost?  
    * Pricing depends on wall length, height, material, ground conditions, and access.  
    * We provide tailored pricing once your project has been assessed.  
  * What types of retaining walls do you build?  
    * We construct:  
      * Concrete sleeper walls  
      * Treated pine sleeper walls  
      * Hardwood timber walls  
    * Concrete sleepers are often preferred for their durability and low maintenance.  
  * Do you do repairs or partial retaining wall work?  
    * No — we focus on full retaining wall installations.  
    * We don’t take on repairs or isolated sections.  
  * Can you reuse existing posts or materials?  
    * No — we install new materials to ensure structural integrity, consistency, and long-term performance.  
  * Can you install a fence on top of an existing retaining wall?  
    * We assess each project as a whole.  
    * In most cases, the retaining wall and fence need to be designed and installed together to ensure stability and long-term performance.  
  * Can you move the retaining wall to gain more land?  
    * Retaining wall placement must align with property boundaries and site constraints.  
    * We build along the agreed boundary and cannot reposition structures beyond this.  
  * Does the retaining wall need to be within my boundary?  
    * Retaining walls are constructed along the agreed boundary between parties.  
    * Any variations must be resolved prior to proceeding.  
  * Can I keep trees or roots in place?  
    * Retaining wall construction requires excavation along the wall line.  
    * Roots and obstructions may need to be removed to allow proper installation.  
    * We can assist with minor roots and small stumps where required.  
  * What should I expect during installation?  
    * Retaining wall construction involves excavation and ground works.  
    * Site conditions such as soil type, rocks, roots, or buried services can impact the installation process.  
    * Final scope may vary depending on conditions encountered during works.  
  * Do you need access to the site?  
    * Yes — clear access is required for machinery, materials, and installation.  
    * Limited access may affect feasibility or require adjustments.  
  * Who is responsible for underground services?  
    * Clients should identify known underground services prior to works.  
    * We take a proactive approach on site, however unknown services can impact the installation process.  
  * Will the wall be perfectly level all the way through?  
    * Retaining walls are set out based on site conditions and design requirements.  
    * In many cases, stepped sections are required rather than a continuous level wall.  
  * Why is there a gap under parts of my retaining wall?  
    * Minor gaps can occur due to ground variation and drainage requirements.  
    * This is a normal outcome in certain site conditions and supports long-term performance.  
  * Do you handle drainage as part of the retaining wall?  
    * We recommend appropriate drainage to support the performance and longevity of the wall.  
    * Requirements vary depending on the site and project.  
  * How long does a retaining wall last?  
    * Lifespan depends on materials and site conditions.  
      * Concrete sleeper walls: 50+ years  
      * Hardwood timber walls: 25–30 years  
      * Treated pine walls: 15–20 years  
    * Proper installation and drainage play a key role in long-term performance.
