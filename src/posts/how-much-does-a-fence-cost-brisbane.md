Getting a new fence in Brisbane? The most common question we get is: *"What's it going to cost?"*

The honest answer is: it depends. But this guide will give you realistic price ranges so you know what to expect before you get a single quote.

## What Affects Fencing Cost?

### 1. Length of the fence
More metres = lower cost per metre (the setup and labour cost is spread). A 50m job costs less per metre than a 15m job.

### 2. Height
Standard residential is 1.8m. Every extra 300mm (e.g. going to 2.1m) adds material and labour cost.

### 3. Site conditions
- **Sloping ground** requires more work to achieve a level or raked fence line
- **Rocky soil** or **concrete footings** to break through add time and cost
- **Difficult access** (narrow side gates, limited truck access) can increase labour

### 4. Removal of existing fencing
Demolition and disposal of an old fence typically adds $8–$15 per metre.

### 5. Gates
Pedestrian gates: **$300–$600 installed** depending on material and style.
Driveway/double gates: **$800–$2,500+** depending on width and automation.

### 6. Material quality
Colorbond® Ultra (corrosion-resistant for coastal areas) costs slightly more than standard. Hardwood timber costs more than treated pine.

## Colorbond® Fencing: What to Expect

A standard 30-metre Colorbond® fence at 1.8m height might cost anywhere from **$2,500 to $4,000** supply and install, depending on the factors above.

The biggest variables:
- Site access
- Ground conditions (flat vs sloped)
- Whether old fencing needs removal

Colorbond® is usually a 1–2 day install for a standard residential job. It's the most quoted material we work with.

---

## Timber Fencing: What to Expect

Treated pine paling fences are comparable in price to Colorbond®. For the same 30m run:
- **Treated pine**: $2,100–$3,300 supply and install
- **Hardwood**: $3,300–$4,800 supply and install

Hardwood costs more but lasts longer with proper maintenance.

---

## Retaining Walls: What to Expect

Retaining walls are priced differently — usually by the linear metre plus the height. A 10m treated pine retaining wall at 1m height might cost **$2,500–$4,000** installed.

Concrete sleeper walls cost more upfront but require virtually no maintenance over their 50+ year lifespan.

Key cost factors for retaining walls:
- Length and height
- Material (treated pine vs hardwood vs concrete)
- Drainage requirements (ag pipe, backfill)
- Whether a fence goes on top
